> [!QUESTION]
> What is the scientific rationale behind the current focus?

The current standard model of cosmology: $\Lambda$CDM assumes a constant dark energy density $(\Lambda)$. From the theoretical standview there is no need to make dark energy cosntant, besides it is a simpler model.

Recent results ([arxiv.org/2503.14743](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.14743)) have proposed that the equation of state (EoS) of DE might be evolving with time. In this project we employ first-year theoretical physics-level tools to analyse the stability of dynamical dark eneergy (DDE) potentials. 

We plan to have 3 tracks, each student can choose their own flabor. Depending on how many students we are this first semester and our interests we may change/add/remove this.

1. **Rolling scalar field (quintessence/exponential):** this is the simplest extension from $\Lambda$CDM
2. **High-energy proxies:** potentials inspired or derived from string theory (RPT, inverse power-law)
3. **PNGB/axiom-like:** potentials linked to observables in the gravitaional wave spectrum.
